The revolution will not be industrialized

  • The Great Industrial Revolution. Chimneys flew up. Production lines erupted into life. Skills swapped for speed. Making was mobilised, mechanised, and centralised. Gone were the days of objects crafted by hand, now things were made by the many.
  • It was hailed the world over for taking humanity forward. For launching an empire. But what if it actually took us back? What if handing over control, creativity and choice to the few, the factories; actually set us down a poorer path?
  • Today's factories are no different. Sure we've got robots, and wares Victorians couldn't even imagine, But they still stifle creativity, Creativity does not come first, even in creative business.
  • After 191 years, isn't it time for a different way? Let’s give back freedom to makers everywhere. Our mission is to lead the charge as we take back making for the masses. Anyone is welcome to join us, people and companies alike.
  • It’s time to start the un-industrial revolution.
